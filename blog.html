DR TEJESWINI MANOGNA

About

Dr. Tejaswini Manogna, born on May 19, 1994, in Hyderabad, India, is a distinguished figure known for her contributions in multiple domains, including medicine, beauty pageantry, classical dance, and yoga. She completed her schooling at Rosary Convent High School in Hyderabad, where she demonstrated exceptional talent from an early age. At just 16 years old, Tejaswini was recognized as ‘India's Best NCC Cadet’ and ‘Best Shooter,’ standing out among 1.3 million cadets across the Indian Air Force, Army, and Navy. Her academic journey led her to Osmania Medical College, where she graduated with a degree in MBBS in 2017. Tejaswini’s pursuit of excellence extended beyond academics into the world of beauty pageants, where she earned recognition for her charisma and intelligence. In 2017, she participated in the prestigious Miss Diva competition, finishing in the top 6 and winning the 'Miss Talented' and 'Best Makeover' awards. Her most significant achievement in pageantry came in 2019 when she was crowned Miss Earth India and represented India at the Miss Earth 2019 pageant in the Philippines. During this competition, she received the 'Miss Global Choice' and 'Eco Trivia' awards, further establishing her as a well-rounded and influential personality. In addition to her beauty queen status, Tejaswini is a trained classical dancer with expertise in Bharatanatyam. She has performed in over 1,500 concerts, including a notable performance before former President Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam. Her dedication to arts and culture is evident through her mastery in dance, and she continues to inspire audiences with her graceful performances. Along with her passion for dance, Tejaswini holds a diploma in yoga training and has been an active yoga instructor in Hyderabad, promoting health and wellness in her community. Tejaswini is also committed to social causes and environmental sustainability. She uses her platform to raise awareness about various issues, particularly in the fields of education and healthcare. She is married to Dr. Pavan Datta, an IAS officer, and together they contribute to societal development in their respective fields.
